Features

Built to remove the temptation, not just the noise.

Kills Slack, keeps it dead

The moment focus begins, Ortus terminates the Slack app. Relaunch it and Ortus re-kills it instantly. There is no notification to peek at because there is no Slack to peek at.

No escape hatch

There's no "End Focus" button. Quitting Ortus is blocked while you're in focus. Emergency end is hidden and limited to once per week.

Scheduled & manual focus

Set recurring focus hours — weekdays 9–12, say — so it just happens. Or start now with a sunrise slider, anywhere from 15 minutes to 4 hours.

Slack status sync

Before it closes Slack, Ortus sets your status and turns on Do Not Disturb, so your team knows you're heads-down. Both clear automatically when focus ends.

AI Slack assistant

Need to catch up without opening the floodgates? Chat with your Slack through your local Claude Code and its Slack MCP — read threads, get summaries, draft replies — all without unblocking the app. No API key is ever stored in Ortus.

Menu-bar native

Lives quietly in your menu bar — no dock icon, no clutter. Pure SwiftUI, zero dependencies, macOS 14 and up.

How it works

Three moments. Zero willpower required.

  1. 1

    Focus activates

    On a schedule you set, or the moment you start a manual session with the slider.

  2. 2

    Slack goes dark

    Ortus sets your status & DND, then terminates Slack — and re-kills it if it dares come back.

  3. 3

    First light returns

FAQ

The honest details.

What if there's a real emergency?

There's a hidden emergency end, deliberately limited to once per calendar week. It exists for genuine emergencies — not for the 3pm urge to "just check one thing."

Does Ortus read my Slack messages?

No. Ortus only sets your status and Do Not Disturb. The optional AI assistant runs through your own local Claude Code and its Slack MCP — Ortus never stores an API key or your message content.

Can't I just quit the app to get Slack back?

No. Quitting Ortus is blocked during an active focus session. That's the whole point — the escape hatch is removed on purpose.

What does it cost?

Nothing. Ortus is free and open source. You can read every line on GitHub and build it yourself.

What are the requirements?

macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. It's a native menu-bar app built in pure SwiftUI with zero third-party dependencies.
